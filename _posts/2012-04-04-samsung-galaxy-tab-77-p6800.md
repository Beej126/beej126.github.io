(My previous go to device, the [Samsung Q1 Ultra](/2008/08/samsung-q1u-umpc.html).&nbsp; She served us well. üôÇ Have we come a long way in 5 _short_ years??? 

* [Samsung‚Äôs Official Landing Page](https://www.samsung.com/global/microsite/galaxytab/7.7/spec.html?type=find) for the gtab 7.7 

Highlights: 

* 7.89mm (0.31 inch) thick &#8211; just amazing to hold 
* 345 grams (0.76 lb) ‚Äì makes for a great eReader‚Ä¶ the brushed Alu back is nicely grippy after your hand has warmed it up for few secs (our Kindle 3G is 247g, 8.5mm and eInk is definitely cool for reading but it‚Äôs not color, smaller screen, lower res, not touch and isn‚Äôt app-tastic. Kindle Fire is 413 g, 11.4mm.) 
* Super AMOLED Plus screen, 1280 x 800 res ‚Äì very pleasing as everyone points out. But to be totally frank, I ride outdoors a lot, and these are really no better in direct sunlight than anything else LCD based‚Ä¶ indoors or even in a casted shadow, the screen is very nice. 
* 1.4GHz ‚Äì everything runs nice and peppy on Android 3.2 
* 3G ‚Äì HSPA+ 
* microSD ‚Äì surprisingly, the [8.9](https://www.samsung.com/us/mobile/galaxy-tab/GT-P7310MAAXAR-specs), [10.1](https://www.samsung.com/global/microsite/galaxytab/10.1/spec.html) sibling tabs do not support an SD card. The original 5‚Äù [Note](https://www.samsung.com/global/microsite/galaxynote/note/spec.html) and [Tab 2](https://www.samsung.com/global/microsite/galaxytab2/index.html) 10.1 & 7.0 do, however. 
* full HD video MPEG4, H.264 play very smooth‚Ä¶ I‚Äôve tried just a few, one MKV wouldn‚Äôt play audio, another would. 
* 5100mAh battery

Some rather obscure downsides I‚Äôve noticed so far: 
                            
* When USB connected to a PC gtab 7.7 does NOT mount as a [mass storage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/USB_mass_storage_device_class) device; It mounts as an [MTP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Media_Transfer_Protocol) device. For one, we don‚Äôt get a Windows drive letter. Drag and drop from Windows Explorer is basically the same‚Ä¶ but various sync tools only work with mass storage. [This post](https://forum.xda-developers.com/showthread.php?t=1128220&page=5) indicates that Honeycomb‚Äôs mixing of filesystems in the system folder structure is what renders mass storage infeasible (to PC‚Äôs). 
* The microSD card natively mounts to /mnt/sdcard/extStorages/SdCard ‚Ä¶ [there doesn‚Äôt appear to be an Android standard established](https://stackoverflow.com/questions/7251793/android-absolute-location-of-external-sd-card) so (some) apps don‚Äôt know where to find it.
